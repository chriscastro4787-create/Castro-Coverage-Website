Common Questions

Everything you want to know

Straight answers. No insurance jargon.

What's the difference between a private plan and an ACA marketplace plan?
Private health plans come directly from insurance carriers, outside the government marketplace. They usually have broader networks and better benefits like $0 deductibles and $0 copays. Marketplace plans can still be a great fit, especially if you qualify for subsidies. We always look at private plans first, then consider marketplace options if they make more sense for your situation.
How much does it cost to work with Castro Coverage?
Nothing at all. Our services are completely free. Insurance carriers pay us when you enroll, so your premium is exactly the same whether you use an advisor or go it alone. No markups, no hidden fees.
Are you independent, or do you only work with certain carriers?
Fully independent. We work with all major carriers including Blue Cross Blue Shield, UnitedHealthcare, Cigna, Aetna, Humana, and more. We also compare marketplace options when they are a stronger fit. No quotas, no incentive to favor any carrier. Our only obligation is to you.
I already have insurance through my employer. Can you still help me?
Absolutely. In many cases, a private or marketplace plan is more cost-effective than employer-sponsored coverage, especially if you qualify for subsidies. We will compare your existing plan against available options so you can make a well-informed decision, with no sales pressure.
How quickly can I get coverage?
Most clients are covered within one to two weeks. If you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period because of a job loss or life event, we can often get you enrolled the same day and have coverage active within days.
What if I need help after I'm enrolled?
We're your long-term advisor, not just your enrollment contact. We assist with claims questions, plan changes, and we proactively reach out before open enrollment each year to review your coverage. You will always have a real, knowledgeable person to call.
